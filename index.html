A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam (Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam) was an Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, Kalam was a renowned aerospace engineer and played a key role in India's civilian space program and military missile development. Dr. Kalam was often referred to as the "People's President" due to his down-to-earth personality and popularity among the masses. Before becoming the President, he held various important positions in India's space and defense organizations. He was a key figure in the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) and ballistic missile technology. Apart from his contributions to science and technology, Abdul Kalam was also a great motivator and speaker, especially among the youth. He was known for his inspirational speeches and writings, emphasizing the importance of education, innovation, and hard work. Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. He left behind a legacy of scientific achievement, leadership, and a commitment to the betterment of society.

Biography:-

    Early Life and Education:

  • Kalam was born into a humble Tamil Muslim family. His father, Jainulabdeen, was a boat owner, and his mother, Ashiamma, was a housewife. Despite financial constraints, Kalam was a hardworking student with a keen interest in science and technology. He studied aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT), and later, he joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).

    • Career in Aerospace Engineering:

  • Abdul Kalam played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development. He was involved in various projects, including the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) and the Indigenous Guided Missile Development Program.
